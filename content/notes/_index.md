# Notes

Short pieces, 300–600 words. Written as I learn. Low polish by design.

These are explicitly **not** authority pieces. They are learning logs —
thinking-in-progress made visible. The git history is the record of how my
thinking changed.

If you —for any reason— cite a note, cite the commit hash. Notes are
editable; the commit hash is what uniquely identifies the version you read.

## Cadence

Roughly weekly, but irregularly. I write notes when something I read or
thought about wants to be written down. I don't force a schedule.

## Format conventions

- Filenames: `YYYY-MM-DD-slug.md`
- Front matter: `title`, `date`, `description`, optional `tags`
- Length: aim for 300–600 words; shorter is fine

## Editing policy

Editable freely. The diff is the record. No revision notes required for
notes — that's what `git log` is for.
